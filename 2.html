Understanding Ecosystem Diversity

Ecosystem diversity refers to the variety of ecosystems in a given place. Ecosystems are communities of organisms and their physical environment interacting as an ecological unit. Different ecosystems such as forests, deserts, grasslands, and aquatic environments all contribute to biodiversity. This rich tapestry of ecosystems provides a multitude of ecological services, from clean air and water to food production and natural resource cycling. Protecting ecosystem diversity is vital for maintaining the health of our planet and ensuring its ability to sustain life.
